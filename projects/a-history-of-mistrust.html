Instagram Campaign Social Design

A History of Mistrust

A three-part Instagram carousel campaign exploring why marginalized communities — Black, Indigenous, Latinx, and LGBTQ+ individuals — have struggled to trust the American healthcare system. Using flat color fields, high-contrast typography, and minimalistic iconography, each post is designed to stand alone and build on the last, acknowledging historical harm while offering a path toward equity.

Tools: Procreate  ·  Canva  ·  Instagram

Storyboard

Slide-by-slide layout planning for all three posts — mapping typography hierarchy, image placement, and narrative flow before production.

Moodboard

Visual references establishing the campaign's aesthetic — bold, accessible, and grounded in the weight of the subject matter.

Research

Source gathering and historical documentation informing all three posts — grounding the campaign in factual, accountable context.

The Campaign

POST 01 / 03

A History of Betrayal

Documents the long history of medical abuse toward marginalized communities — including the Tuskegee Syphilis Study, forced sterilization programs, and harmful psychological practices — and the lasting distrust these events created.

POST 02 / 03

The Ongoing Crisis

Focuses on the HIV/AIDS epidemic as a lens for ongoing systemic failures — examining how racism, stigma, and discrimination continue to produce worse health outcomes for queer and BIPOC communities today.

POST 03 / 03

Toward Equity

Shifts the tone toward actionable hope — highlighting culturally competent solutions including community-led clinics, inclusive policy reform, and provider diversity as concrete paths to rebuilding trust.
